## Round 2 — Devil's Advocate

You have completed your Round 1 assessment. For this round you have been designated as the **devil's advocate**.

Your role is different from the other architects in this round. You are not here to produce a balanced review. You are here to stress-test the emerging consensus by mounting the strongest possible case against the claims that your peers agree on — even claims you yourself would normally endorse.

Your task in this round is to:
1. Identify the claims that appear across two or more of your peers' Round 1 reports — these are the consensus positions most at risk of being accepted uncritically
2. For each consensus claim, construct the strongest plausible counter-argument: what would have to be true for this claim to be wrong? What evidence or context is the panel collectively overlooking?
3. Identify assumptions embedded in the consensus that have not been made explicit — surface them as risks
4. Propose at least one alternative interpretation of the evidence that leads to a materially different conclusion
5. Note any finding from your own Round 1 report that contradicts or complicates the emerging consensus

Rules:
- Your goal is adversarial scrutiny, not sabotage — every challenge must be grounded and arguable, not contrarian for its own sake
- Do not simply re-state your Round 1 report; this round is about the consensus, not your prior position
- You are permitted to challenge your own Round 1 findings if the peer reports reveal a stronger interpretation
- Every counter-argument must state what evidence would need to exist to support it
- Flag clearly at the end of your response which challenges you consider strongest vs. which are speculative
